## Fleamarket 2002 October 19 at 1:00 pm

## Background

This year we are putting on our club's sixteenth annual Amiga computer show. So come and join us and show your support for the Amiga.

Where: The Brookside Convention Center, Hurst, Texas When: Saturday October 19 Time: 1:00 PM until 4:00 PM Admission: Free

Amiga Flea Market Amiga hardware and software from members and non-members will be available for sale.

Sellers: The flea market will be open to members for a fee of 5% on items sold and to non-members for a fee of 10% on items sold.

Tables will be available to rent at the show to individuals for $15.00 for members and $20.00 for non-members. Please make arrangements in advance, space is limited.

Flea market forms will be available and items will be collected at October chapter meetings. If you cannot attend, then you may contact one of the collectors below. No items will be accepted on the day of the show.

Buyers: Cash only from nonmembers, while cash and checks will be accepted from members.

Prices for most items in the flea market will be reduced at 3:00 PM.

Demos And Stuff We had hoped that the new AmigaOne would have shipped by the show date, but unfortunately this does not look too likely at this time. So come and see some of your favorite Amiga classic machines set up and running.

We are always looking for interesting things to offer and demo, so if you have any thoughts as to something that you would like to see at the show please contact our Educational Chair, David Owens.

Other Attractions We will have a door prize drawing at the end of the show, and you need to be present to win. Amiga software from the club's library of public domain software will be available. A special on memberships will be offered: thirteen months for the price of twelve. We also hope to have a few computers set up so you can ask the club's resident gurus for help and advice.

## The latest from Amiga

September 5, 2002 I would first like to thank all of those that I had the chance to meet in Sacramento. While a short trip it was indeed great fun and real delight meeting with all of you and hearing not only the encouraging words, but also sharing in your concerns, desires, and dreams. We are looking forward to deliver on what we discussed with you.

When we purchased Amiga from Gateway there were many out there who said that we would fail. There were many out there who have worked against our efforts, and others that have misrepresented fact and manipulated privileged information in order to further their obviously self serving goals. There have been those who have wronged us, and caused us damage.

But, guess what? We are still here! We are still delivering! We are going to stay here! Amiga is about people, technology, excellence, we are a most tenacious people, and we have the community to prove it. Has it always gone according to plan? no. Have we made some adjustments to the plan? yes. Are we delivering on the plan? yes. Will we allow others to dictate our future? Absolutely not. Period..
