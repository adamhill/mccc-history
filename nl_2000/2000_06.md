## AGM Show Announced

## Our 14 th annual computer show

I  am  pleased  to  announce the club's fourteenth annual computer show.

Date: Saturday October 14 Time: 12:00 PM until 4:00 PM Place: The Brookside Convention Center Cost: Free

## DIRECTIONS &amp; PARKING

The  Brookside  Convention  Center  is  located  at  1244 Brookside Drive in Hurst. To get to the Convention Center get to Highway 183/121 and take the Precinct Line Road exit and drive south on Precinct Line Road. After approximately 0.3 miles turn left (east), on Bedford Euless Road. Drive  0.4  miles  to  Brookside  Drive.  The  Convention Center is on the southeast corner.

## FLEA MARKET

The flea market will be open to members for a fee of 5% on  items  sold.  A  fee  of  10%  will  be  charged  to  nonmembers on items sold. The club is considering letting members and non-members having tables of their own for a small fee. Flea market forms will be available at the July chapter meetings. Items will be collected during chapter meetings  beginning  in  August.  If  you  cannot  attend  a meeting, contact a designated collector. A list of collectors will be in the July newsletter.

## OTHER ATTRACTIONS

We will have a door prize drawing at the end of the show. You need to be present to win. The club's software library for the Amiga will be available to members. A special on memberships  will  be  offered:  thirteen  months  for  the price of twelve. We also hope to have a few computers set up so you can ask the club's resident gurus for help and advice.

Your assistance is necessary to make the show a success. If you can help, call me at metro (972) 299-6824, or leave email at mstodola@airmail.net.

## 15 Years Ago

## From the MCCC News

On May 10-12, Commodore wizard, James Butterfield, came  to  the  Metroplex  under  the  sponsorship  of  the MCCC and Scope User Groups. Meeting at the Western Hills  Inn,  the  events  included  an  informal  banquet,  an entertaining lecture and learning sessions on topcs from file  handling to machine language. We were also introduced to the new C128 by Pete Baczor (Commodore's User Group Coordinator) who also provided answers to a variety  of  questions  from  the  floor.  Perhaps  the  most important piece of information we learned was the tollfree  phone  number  for  Customer  Support:  1-800-2479000.

## A M I G A / C O R E L   A   W I N N E R Major support for the new Amiga

Snoqualmie, WA, April 1, 2000 - Amiga Incorporated, is pleased to announce that its new multi-media consumer interface is well supported by Corel LINUX OS and other Corel applications including the newly released WordPerfect Office 2000 for Linux.

Amiga's multi-media consumer interface runs extremely smooth and fast on Corel LINUX OS, and the compatibility  between  these  great  products  has  provided  Linux developers with a new and exciting way to create incredible multi-media titles, and games for Linux.

'Amiga pioneered the creation of multi-media, and easy to use graphical interfaces 20 years ago, and today begins the  path  of  the  reintroduction  of  a  legend.  The  New Amiga  is  the  creation  and  embrace  of  leading  technologies  and  combining  them  with  the  20  years  of experience in multi- media development environments,' said Bill McEwen, president and chief executive officer of Amiga.  'Corel  has  leading  products  within  their categories,  and  we  are  excited  about  their  supporting Amiga towards the future.'

'Linux developers now have choices when it comes to leading edge applications,' added McEwen.
